Steemit Statistics - 2017-07-04

https://i.cubeupload.com/QQ1wb2.jpg

Welcome to my daily analytical report about Steemit.


All the information presented in this report are based on the data collected from the blockchain until 2017-07-04 midnight UTC time. The goal of this report is to provide you with a clear view on what is happening daily on Steemit.

1. New users

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-AccountsNew.png

This chart shows the number of newly registered users on Steemit and how many of them became active. We see that a lot of accounts are registered, but never used.

Warning: this graphic is kind of dynamic! A user can register one day and become a few days later. Therefore, the number of active users on a specific date may change from day to day.

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Accounts.png

In this graph, the orange line shows the total number of registered accounts. The green line shows how many of them have been active at least once (by active, I mean those who made at least one post, comment or upvote). They are included even if they become inactive later.

2. Post and comments

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Posts.png https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Posts30.png

These graphs show the evolution of posts and comments for the whole blockchain lifetime and for the last 30 days.

3. Curation

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Votes.png https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Votes30.png

These graphs show the evolution of curation (upvotes) for the whole blockchain lifetime and for the last 30 days

4. Active users

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Actives.png https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Actives30.png

These graphs show the activity of users over time and in more details for the last 30 days.

5. Daily transactions

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Transactions.png

This graph shows the number of daily transactions. This give you an idea of the whole activity on the blockchain.

6. Categories

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Categories.png

This graph shows the tag has been the most used for publishing posts for the whole blockchain lifetime.

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-CategoriesDay.png

This graph shows the tag has been the most used for publishing posts last day.

7. Distribution

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Reputation.png

This graph shows the distribution of the reputation among users. Accounts with a reputation lower than 25 have been removed to keep the graph readable.

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-Levels.png

This graph shows the distribution of the voting power (it now includes the new "Inactive" and "Red Fish" levels - check @steemitboard blog for more info about this).

8. Payout evolution

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-PayoutMax.png https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-PayoutMax30.png

These graphs show the maximum reward paid on a post (or comment) for each day (whole blockchain life and last 30 days).

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-PayoutTotal.png https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-PayoutTotal30.png

These graphs show the total of the distributed payout (posts and comments) for each day.

https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-PayoutAVG30.png https://arcange.eu/steem-images/2017-07-04-PayoutMedianPosts30.png

These graphs show the average and median payout per post for the last 30 days. The last one indicates that if yesterday you got the latest value shown on this chart for your post, you should be happy because there are as much people that have earned more than you than people that have earned less than you.

I hope you find those stats useful. If you would like to see different stats, feel free to drop me a comment. Your feedback is more than welcome.

Thanks for reading.
