Broken White Line ![](https://steemitimages.com/DQmQmJh2VCkEP1g672QpbE7nwVAvy2M5HXTvFHwKByEi5bu/image.png) The most common of road markings in India, a broken white line indicates that you may change lanes, overtake vehicles or make a U-turn after seeing that it is safe to do so. Solid White Line : ![](https://steemitimages.com/DQmRajTTg9xTw41iXCuGuCV3kGvcV7r6byzpVs3CsoSmozR/image.png) You cannot overtake a vehicle or make a U-turn if your on a road with a continuous white line. However, you may cross the line to avoid an obstruction or to exit the road to enter a perpendicular road. Double Yellow Lines - Road Markings in India : ![](https://steemitimages.com/DQmQ6h5Ejxw4ccuZptwdJZUS8NpgS4fkbyZRvVXNScqnvaq/image.png) Double Yellow lines can never be crossed unless it is to avoid a major obstruction. Single Yellow Line next to a Broken Yellow Line : ![](https://steemitimages.com/DQmY3DLd1dom4PZ2mVaYriTbbbU6CXBEm4fZv1dwxpSfJeC/image.png) This type of road marking combines the rules of the first two markings in this list. If you are driving on the side with the broken line, you are allowed to overtake and if youâ€™re driving on the side of the solid line, youâ€™re not.     Travel with me #78 : A visit to the magnificent Rose Island, Sandy Toes and snorkeling with Lion Fish!

    July 13, 2017

    ### Dear Steemit friends : ![IMG_0548.jpg](https://steemitimages.com/DQmY8pnFGdtBcQXtoLhTD1i8XhLzqvyqcyy45vAz32wUJWG/IMG_0548.jpg) One of the best things about Bahamas is how many beautiful exotic islands there are. In fact, there are over 700 islands and islets which constitute the country that is Bahamas. Each have their own unique pedigree but I'm sure most visitors to Bahamas will agree that they are all largely unspoiled and very picturesque! I haven't had much time to explore all the islands on this trip, but I did have the opportunity to visit the main ones like [Compass Cay](https://steemit.com/travel/@sweetsssj/travel-with-me-76-swimming-with-the-sharks-at-compass-cay), [Major Cay](https://steemit.com/travel/@sweetsssj/travel-with-me-72-visiting-major-cay-and-taking-a-swim-with-the-pigs) and [Exuma Cays](https://steemit.com/travel/@sweetsssj/travel-with-me-71-powerboating-to-exuma-cays-feeding-the-iguanas-photos-video). Each had their own little surprises which are definitely worth a visit! With so much to see and do, you might be wondering whether I had the opportunity to just enjoy the tropical weather, chill on the beach, swim in the sea and relax? The answer to that is yes! I had the pleasure of visiting ***Rose Island, Sandy Toes*** and spend a day sipping cocktails by the beach, and swimming in the sea! Many would say that's the ideal activity for such a vacation and I must say - I agree! https://image.ibb.co/dS0dqF/IMG_8023.jpg ![DSC02068.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmQ81pNb12ovYRUMaYeZrdQfYaW5dwpdMqohPSyAUkHKnr/DSC02068.JPG) The day started very early, I woke up around 7am to another gorgeous day brimming with sunshine and was ready for a lazy relaxing day at the private island known for it's pink sands! The excursion was very easy to book, you can either book at your hotel, over the internet, or just turn up at the port where the boat leaves. Of course, I like to make sure everything is booked up in advance so I booked my excursion the very first day I arrived. https://image.ibb.co/cZWUxv/IMG_8022.jpg ![DSC02046.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmSQda931aEthBzm7gEVtZSV9kNhCUCLy54SaC8wdMWzXm/DSC02046.JPG) The gathering point for the boat trip is Magaritaville, a very popular restaurant and bar at the edge of Paradise Island. ![DSC02043.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmZSXw31d8ZKvC69xuXnnwPmHtisRNqz7eKkCfJfAZJP3g/DSC02043.JPG) Just beside Magaritaville, is the port where all of the boats are docked ready to take people all around Bahamas. The bridge in the picture connects Nassau to Paradise Island and is quite impressive! http://image.ibb.co/kUEvKa/IMG_0497.jpg For this trip, a speedboat wasn't necessary because there were probably around 50 other people coming as well. The boat gently pulls out from the dock and makes it's way at moderate speed through the harbour towards the open seas. ![DSC02050.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmZ7QqB5mHUfizmGg5UNpgsMuZGXCwmMhseZjAF1ytM9Wm/DSC02050.JPG) Only 15 minutes later, we're already arriving at Rose Island. The boat trip was short and actually very pleasant. Before docking, we drove along the long elongated island and saw very few man made buildings there. It certainly looks like a paradise waiting for me to explore! ![DSC02085.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmf8eWHbHSJS1HjPn6ahy8rRy9EH76PWRdTxBDtbNbMYHB/DSC02085.JPG) The boat pulls over at what looks like the only dock on the island and we all disembark the boat immediately clamouring up the side of the hill ready to explore. ![DSC02096.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmPYQBMwSuZT7g5GjcmyYifm3ynEZ6HREnhx2wzS5K8H4U/DSC02096.JPG) http://image.ibb.co/k7rLkF/IMG_0498.jpg ![DSC02097.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmaLXTQnd2w5fKPTgx5hb56VM3ABxqRf3BHazrv5WLAYh7/DSC02097.JPG) http://image.ibb.co/nfJpea/IMG_0499.jpg To my surprise, I was greeted by a bunch of free roaming chickens! ![DSC02102.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQme9z7cWH4FzoSR7BAiKSAc76ADUFSCYwHbPffZ2wt8hjf/DSC02102.JPG) A small narrow passage connects between the landing area and the beach. It's all very natural and walking through, I felt like I was trekking through a small forest with Chickens watching my every step. How peculiar! http://image.ibb.co/hpdpea/IMG_0500.jpg Amazingly, there were also quite a number of peacocks just chilling in the shade. Coming so close to them, I'm amazed by their beautiful plumage. They are even more beautiful in person than the pictures could show. ![DSC02113.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmZ3nwq8nqGb9hR41pXxaLdMAYR5cBFP9BSqLcDAPQpLQD/DSC02113.JPG) http://image.ibb.co/dM46QF/IMG_0501.jpg ![DSC02116.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmVo2Z2yEfyybybqHShzVWHCGsefibq2WjdbtWDurhVojP/DSC02116.JPG) http://image.ibb.co/nr6xXv/IMG_0502.jpg ![DSC02117.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmWvCsnsn9YASwZ3rfrtbnYjdrjzoG1kvJ9coEERvkt6Ap/DSC02117.JPG) ![DSC02120.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmSRpKAKkCXXvsrHGKE7M3V8iB5ADJ72uCvomXBXkkzPPt/DSC02120.JPG) At the end of the little passage, we arrive at the beach bar and massage area which overlooks the sea and beautiful beach. http://image.ibb.co/mUiNza/IMG_0503.jpg As you can imagine, i'm a little anxious to get my my feet wet! http://image.ibb.co/hOarsv/IMG_0504.jpg I was really blown away by the beautiful and natural setting. All around, there are these beautiful rock formations that have been left untouched preserving their beauty. In the distance, you can see a neighbouring island like a mirage unveiled before your eyes. ![DSC02269.JPG](https://steemitimages.com/DQmdwnN8emJyehBWovc8QoFBAvvQm4MdLzaAE8Q38e8A8Ez/DSC02269.JPG) One of the best aspects about Rose Island is that it is a private island. This means there are virtually no people there and you can have so much of the beach to yourself. Exclusivity aside, the name ***Rose Island*** also comes from the fact that here, you will find the sandy beaches tinted in a slightly Rose colour. Sometimes, it's even called ***"Pink Sands"***. http://image.ibb.co/eJjh0F/IMG_8708.jpg The skies were absolutely amazing, whilst the sun toasted my skin and added to my already darkening skin tone, I kicked back for a moment to enjoy the neon-blue skies, threaded by heavenly white puffy clouds. http://image.ibb.co/eD6vLF/IMG_8695.jpg With such scenic and idyllic surroundings, it's hard to resist jumping for joy... http://image.ibb.co/gyTY5F/IMG_0506.jpg Before long, I felt the need to take a dip in the calm and tranquil water. Like most other islands in Bahamas, the water clarity is something to me marvelled at. http://image.ibb.co/k9nXza/IMG_0529.jpg Just around this rock formation, you can see the coral reefs through the crystal clear water. It's amazing how the water blends from a yellow colour and through a gradient all the way to the tiffany blue colour you see further out. http://image.ibb.co/kYV0nv/IMG_8707.jpg Perfect opportunity for some on the fly photoshoots! http://image.ibb.co/eDraKa/IMG_0510.jpg http://image.ibb.co/hoBUfF/IMG_8702.jpg http://image.ibb.co/frjjCv/IMG_0523.jpg I really can't hold down my excitement so more jumping is due! http://image.ibb.co/iLq2za/IMG_0516.jpg http://image.ibb.co/i3YBsv/IMG_0515.jpg http://image.ibb.co/d152za/IMG_0513.jpg After some time at the beach, the hosts kindly invited us over for some impromptu island lunch. Most people were happy to sit in the shade, but look at that view from the balcony! ![IMG_8727.jpg](https://steemitimages.com/DQmYoJ5iHSWybWiipGXaemtcrUyJ8c5haiUko7HDRqv8hBe/IMG_8727.jpg) http://image.ibb.co/cFWqnv/IMG_8739.jpg http://image.ibb.co/j1bO7v/IMG_8738.jpg http://image.ibb.co/cNbZEa/IMG_8737.jpg http://image.ibb.co/hRPbSv/IMG_8736.jpg http://image.ibb.co/fHSVnv/IMG_8735.jpg http://image.ibb.co/mFPMua/IMG_8734.jpg http://image.ibb.co/bOpnZa/IMG_8733.jpg After lunch, we had a couple more hours to enjoy the beach, but for more the more adventurous of us, we had the option of doing some light snorkelling around the island. http://image.ibb.co/jQMaKa/IMG_0512.jpg The ideal snorkelling spot is just around that rock where the corals and reefs are.

If or when you visit Bahamas, I highly recommend taking one day to enjoy this little gem of an island for a truly memorable day of pure relaxation!
